Complex Analysis

Complex Numbers

A number of the form x + iy where x, y are real numbers and i2 = -1 is called a complex number.

Modulus and Argument of a Complex Number

The modulus of a complex number z = x + iy is the real number √(x2 + y2) and is denoted by |z|.

The argument of a complex number z = x + iy is given by: arg(z) = θ = tan-1(y/x), where x, y ≠ 0.

Complex Functions

In complex analysis, a complex function is a function defined from complex numbers to complex numbers.

Limits of Complex Functions

Let w = f(z) be any function of z defined in a bounded closed domain D. Then the limit of f(z) as z approaches z0 is denoted by “l”.

Continuity of Complex Functions

A complex function w = f(z) defined in the bounded closed domain D, is said to be continuous at a point Z0, if f(z0) is defined and the limit of f(z) as z approaches z0 exists and is equal to f(z0).

Complex Differentiation

Some of the standard results of complex differentiation:

  • dc/dz = 0; where c is a complex constant
  • d/dz (f ± g) = (df/dz) ± (dg/dz)
  • d/dz [c.f(z)] = c . (df/dz)
  • d/dz zn = nzn-1
  • d/dz (f.g) = f (dg/dz) + g (df/dz)
  • d/dz (f/g) = [g (df/dz) – f (dg/dz)]/ g2
